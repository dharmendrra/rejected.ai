What it is

An interview engine that defends every number it produces

Most automated screening collapses a candidate into keyword overlap. rejected.ai instead runs a conversation, extracts concept-linked evidence from each answer, and recomputes confidence over the entire ledger after every turn — so the assessment is explainable, self-correcting, and honest about what it can and cannot measure.

🧾

Evidence over keywords

Each answer yields positive/negative evidence tied to specific competencies. The evidence ledger is the source of truth — every score cites the turns that justify it.

⏪

Retroactive re-scoring

Confidence is never frozen. When a later answer reinterprets an earlier shorthand one, the earlier evidence is revised — with the revision logged for replay.

🌡️

Three confidence lenses

Every competency is scored cool (skeptical), normal (balanced), and hot (generous), so a reader sees the range of defensible interpretations, not a single opaque verdict.

👥

Multi-evaluator panel

An Architect, EM, Staff+, Operator, ATS, Comms, and AI-Native persona each assess independently — surfacing where reasonable evaluators would disagree.

📏

Measurable, never inferred

Audio and video produce only counted signals — pace, fillers, engagement, attention. The system deliberately refuses to infer honesty, intelligence, or personality.

📈

Cross-interview learning

A candidate's competency trajectory across interviews is computed deterministically — pure arithmetic over stored scores, no LLM, fully explainable.

The defining behavior

The evidence loop

Each turn feeds a recompute over the full ledger. Because the whole history is re-evaluated every time, re-scoring earlier answers in light of later ones is structural — not a bolt-on.

Question → Evidence → Confidence update → New evidence → Retroactive re-scoring

…repeated per turn, with a confidence snapshot captured each time for replay.

Why it self-corrects

Confidence isn't accumulated incrementally — it's recomputed from scratch over the full evidence ledger on every turn. That makes retroactive re-scoring a property of the design, so a terse early "duplicate handling" can be lifted once a later answer explains idempotency and exactly-once semantics.

Why it's explainable

Every competency score, strongest signal, risk, and the final hiring recommendation carries citations to the exact turns behind it. The report includes score-evolution sparklines and a revision log — you can audit how a number moved and why.

How it's built

A layered Go engine, one concern per package

Seventeen small, single-responsibility packages behind a stdlib net/http router. LLM generation, embeddings, and the database all sit behind interfaces, so backends swap by config rather than code change.

Design decisions worth noting

Interface seams

One Caller / Streamer / Embedder contract; Ollama and Anthropic are interchangeable implementations. The whole engine is testable without a live model.

Recompute, don't accumulate

Confidence is derived from the entire ledger each turn. Choosing recomputation over incremental updates is what makes retroactive correction free and consistent.

Honest by construction

Measurable signals are modelled as counts and percentages in the type system — there is no field in which an inferred trait could be stored.

Multimodal, but disciplined

Signals it measures — and the ones it refuses to

Audio and video are first-class inputs, but the platform draws a hard ethical line: it reports only what can be counted, and never dresses up inference as measurement.

SourceMeasured (counted)Never inferred
Text / answersConcept-linked evidence, compression ratio, clarification vs. deflection—
AudioSpeaking pace (WPM), filler stats, word count, response latencyhonesty · intelligence
VideoEngagement, attention, participation, timing — as frame percentagespersonality · mood · traits

Both audio and video accept either pre-computed metrics (always available) or a raw upload processed by a pluggable engine — a clean two-path design that keeps the core dependency-light and the signals defensible.
